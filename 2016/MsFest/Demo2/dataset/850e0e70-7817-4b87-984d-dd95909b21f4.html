Quebec police suspend search for missing man

Anne Sutherland, Montreal Gazette
More from Anne Sutherland, Montreal Gazette
Published on: October 21, 2014 | Last Updated: October 23, 2014 12:01 PM EST
Share Adjust Comment Print

UPDATE Oct. 23, 2014: Police suspended their search for a man missing in Matane since Saturday. They had been searching for the man for the past five days using a helicopter, sniffer dogs, all-terrain-vehicles, 40 officers and 20 volunteers. “The man went hunting when it was 19C and that night it dropped to 4C,” said Sgt. Claude Doiron with the Sûrété du Québec. “He didn’t have any food or matches to make a fire.”

The 69-year-old man from Blainville was hunting moose. The park in Matane is 1,200 square kilometres, Doiron said. Officers are still advising volunteers and other hunters to be on the lookout for any clues to the missing hunter’s whereabouts.

UPDATE Oct. 21, 2014: The body of the man who disappeared in Lac Grégoire has been recovered. The body of the man missing in Newport has been found. It appears to be a suicide.

Oct. 20, 2014:

With the rising sun, searching has resumed for three missing men, two hunters and one man believed to have been in a lake in Lanaudière.

One hunter disappeared in Matane, the other failed to meet his family on Sunday afternoon in Newport in the Eastern Townships.

Divers with the Sûreté du Québec have resumed looking for the body of a man believed to have drowned in Lac Grégoire Sunday afternoon.

Police received a 911 call at 3:15 p.m. saying that a man had been spotted calling for help before he went under.

No boat or body was found and the search was stopped with nightfall and resumed on Monday.
